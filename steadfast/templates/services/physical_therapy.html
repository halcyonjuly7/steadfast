Physical Therapy

Our physical therapist (PT) performs therapeutic exercises related to a specific loss of function and involves the utilization of adaptive equipment and aid in rehabilitation. The PT will assess the rehabilitation needs and potential of the patient including the development or implementation of a PT home exercise program.

An appropriate and medically necessary PT program may offer the following benefits:

  1. Increased flexibility
  2. Muscle strengthening
  3. Improved mobility
  4. Reduction of pain
  5. Reduce the risk of falling

PT services may include:
  1. Evaluation of equipment needs and teachings for the safe use of the equipment
  2. Evaluation of gait, ambulation and home safety
  3. Mobility, gait, transfer and prosthetic training
  4. Post-operative rehabilitation and education
  5. herapeutic exercise
  6. Weight-bearing exercise
  7. Written home exercises
A physical therapy assistant (PTA), who is under the supervision of a PT, can perform follow-up treatments.
