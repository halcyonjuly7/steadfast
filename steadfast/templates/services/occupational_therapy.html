Occupational Therapy

Our occupational therapist work closely with our patient's physician and assist patients in developing maximum self-care activities directed toward the return of a former function such as

  • dressing
  • eating
  • toileting
  • bathing

The occupational therapist may formulate activities that will restore the patient's ability to perform Activities of Daily Living (ADL) and Independent Activities of Daily Living (IADL) like:

  1. making adaptive eating utensils and dressing devices
  2. fashioning home exercise programs designed to promote ADL retraining
  3. activities that will develop and improve motor skills training
  4. implementing a neuro-developmental sensory treatment and
  5. Devising a perceptual motor training
